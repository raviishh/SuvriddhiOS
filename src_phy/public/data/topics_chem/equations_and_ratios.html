Stoichiometry

Part 2 — Balanced Equations and Mole Ratios

What a Balanced Equation Actually Says

A chemical equation is more than just a description of what reacts. The coefficients (the numbers in front of each formula) represent exact mole ratios. This is what makes stoichiometry calculations possible.

Law of Conservation of Mass

Atoms are neither created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction — they're only rearranged. This means the total mass of reactants must equal the total mass of products, and the number of each type of atom must be the same on both sides of a balanced equation.

Reading a Balanced Equation

2H₂ + O₂ → 2H₂O

This says:

• 2 moles of H₂ react with 1 mole of O₂ to produce 2 moles of H₂O

• Mole ratio H₂ : O₂ : H₂O = 2 : 1 : 2

• If you have 4 mol H₂, you need 2 mol O₂ and produce 4 mol H₂O

• If you have 0.5 mol O₂, you need 1 mol H₂ and produce 1 mol H₂O

The coefficients scale linearly. You can multiply or divide the whole equation by any number and the ratios stay the same.

Balancing Equations

To balance, adjust coefficients (never subscripts — changing subscripts changes the substance) until atom counts match on both sides.

Strategy:

1. Balance elements that appear in only one compound on each side first.

2. Balance polyatomic ions (SO₄²⁻, NO₃⁻) as units if they appear unchanged.

3. Balance H and O last (they appear in many places).

4. Clear fractions by multiplying all coefficients by the LCM.

The Three-Step Stoichiometry Method

Every mass calculation in stoichiometry follows the same three steps, no exceptions:

Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3

Step 1: Mass → Moles   n = m / M

Step 2: Apply mole ratio   from balanced equation

Step 3: Moles → Mass   m = n × M

Example: Mass of CO₂ from Burning Propane

C₃H₈ + 5O₂ → 3CO₂ + 4H₂O

How many grams of CO₂ are produced from 44 g of propane (C₃H₈)?

Step 1: M(C₃H₈) = 3(12) + 8(1) = 44 g/mol  →  n(C₃H₈) = 44/44 = 1 mol

Step 2: Ratio C₃H₈ : CO₂ = 1 : 3  →  n(CO₂) = 1 × 3 = 3 mol

Step 3: M(CO₂) = 12 + 32 = 44 g/mol  →  m(CO₂) = 3 × 44 = 132 g

Always write out all three steps. The mole ratio step is where most marks are — show which ratio you used and why.

State symbols in equations: (s) = solid, (l) = liquid, (g) = gas, (aq) = aqueous (dissolved in water). These tell you the physical state and are required for full marks in many IB questions.
